Virtual & Augmented Reality

Virtual and Augmented Reality, the next stepping stone in Project Visualization

by Godfrey Muhinda
October 22, 2022
Virtual Reality

As the world continuously gears itself to an ever-changing technological environment, Virtual Reality stands out as a platform that aims to revolutionize industries across the world. Virtual Reality, a term limited to the use of computers, is an alternate reality that is similar to the reality or real world that we experience on a day to day basis. Virtual Reality, an alternate environment, enables users to engage in unlimited imagination that is not bound to real life constraints such as the force of gravity, wind e.t.c. Virtual Reality enables users to create their own worlds that can suite them. A world where anything is possible. The flexibility and growing potential of virtual reality has led to a steady increase in the use of this technology ever since its inception, leading to its application in various industries, from the Architectural Engineering and Construction Industry, to the Education sector, and all the way down to the creation of Games and recreational activities such as museums. The application of this technology has proved to be broad, encompassing all areas of our waking life.

Augmented Reality

Whatever you can see, feel and touch, is your reality. What if it was possible to add objects/elements into your reality, cutting out the aspect of being able to feel and touch them, but at the same time still see them? This is Augmented Reality. Augmented Reality, as the term suggests, is changing the current reality that we can see, feel and touch but including virtual objects/elements that were not there before. In AR systems, the user views the local real-world environment, but the system augments that view with virtual objects.


Types of VR Systems

Virtual Reality consists of systems that come about as a result of several factors such as differences in the display hardware, graphics rendering algorithms, level of user involvement, and level of integration with the physical world.

Immersive VR

As the term suggests, Immersive VR allows the user to step into an environment that creates an illusion of “being there”, whilst still maintaining some contact with the physical/natural world. Immersive VR is mainly achieved using a Head Mounted Display unit, that when put on, displays a virtual environment around the user giving the impression of “a new world”, completely separate from the physical world. This system also incorporates the use of 3D interaction techniques based on whole body input, i.e. to turn left, one must turn their whole body to the left. Additional devices such as joysticks (which represent the virtual hands in Immersive VR) are added to give the user the ability to interact fully with the environment by providing options such as “virtual touch”, “virtual throw” and “virtual grab”. Tracking systems are vital in these systems in-order to capture these movements. This immersion involves “sight and hearing”, and not “taste and feel”. Future improvements to this will involve the inclusion of being able to “taste and feel” the environment.
